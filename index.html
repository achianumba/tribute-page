Chinua Achebe

Author, Professor, and Social Critic
Often hailed as The Patriach of African Literature, Achebe was best known for his unsentimental literary works which depict the psychological and social challenges that traditional and modern Africans had to contend with as a result of the imposition of western culture on traditional African societies.

  • 1930 - Born Albert Chinualumogu Achebe in Ogidi, Anambra.
  • 1936 - School begins. Achebe enrolls at St. Philips' Central School, Ogidi.
  • 1942 - Achebe leaves home to study at the Central School in Nekede.
  • 1944 - Gains admission to study at Government College, Umuahia.
  • 1948 - Beings to study medicine at University College, Ibadan (now University of Ibadan) on scholarship. He later abandons medicine to study English, History, and Theology.
  • 1954 - Achebe joins the Nigerian Broadcasting service.
  • 1958 - Heinemann publishes Achebe’s most famous novel –Things Fall Apart.
  • 1961 - Marries Christiana Chinwe Okolie.
  • 1967 - Co-founds Citadel Press at Enugu with Christopher Okigbo as co-founder.
  • 1967 - Becomes an ambassador of the now defunct Republic of Biafra.
  • 1990 - Begins 18-year tenure as the Charles P. Stevenson Professor of Languages and Literature.
  • 2004 - Declines national honour from the Nigerian government.
  • 2007 - Awarded the Man Booker International Prize.
  • 2009 - Starts serving as a Professor of Africana Studies at Brown University and David and Marianna Fisher University.
  • 2010 - Wins the Dorothy and Lillian Gish Prize (2010).
  • 2011 - Rejects national honour from the Nigerian government, again.
  • 2012 - Publishes his biography –There was a Country: A personal History of Biafra.
  • 2013 - Dies in Boston, Massachusetts.
